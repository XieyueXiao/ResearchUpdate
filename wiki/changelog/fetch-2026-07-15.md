## Papers Found (2026-07-15)

### 1. Microbiota-derived short-chain fatty acids mediate Candida albicans gastrointestinal colonization resistance
- **Journal:** Cell Host & Microbe (IF ~30)
- **Highlights:** Immunocompromised patients are vulnerable to invasive candidiasis seeded from gut colonization, but the mechanisms restraining gut Candida albicans overgrowth are incompletely understood. Mishra et al. show that microbiota-derived short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs) — butyrate, propionate, and acetate — directly inhibit C. albicans growth by forcing fungal metabolic reprogramming, impairing hexose uptake, and inducing intracellular acidification. Mice treated with anti-anaerobic antibiotics showed significantly higher GI C. albicans colonization alongside significantly lower cecal SCFA levels, and SCFA supplementation enhanced colonization resistance only in the presence of an intact gut microbiome, which itself drove SCFA-induced taxonomic shifts. The findings identify microbiota-derived SCFAs as a key colonization-resistance mechanism against gut fungal overgrowth, with implications for preventing invasive candidiasis in at-risk patients.
- **Link:** [DOI: 10.1016/j.chom.2026.05.007](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.chom.2026.05.007)
