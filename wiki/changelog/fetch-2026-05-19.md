### 1. Antibiotic-associated *Enterococcus* expansion in the gastrointestinal tract precedes infected necrosis in acute necrotizing pancreatitis

- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Published:** 2026-05-18
- **Authors:** Fons F. van den Berg et al. (Amsterdam University Medical Center / Department of Medical Microbiology)
- **Highlights:** Using prospective multicenter POEMA cohort data, van den Berg et al. demonstrate that gastrointestinal expansion of *Enterococcus* spp. is a temporally antecedent event to infected pancreatic necrosis in acute necrotizing pancreatitis (ANP). Antibiotic exposure — rather than the inflammatory environment of pancreatitis per se — emerged as the primary driver of *Enterococcus* overgrowth: broad-spectrum antibiotics suppress competing Gram-negative microbiota, creating an ecological vacuum that *Enterococcus* rapidly fills via competitive release. The clinical implication is double-edged: prophylactic antibiotic use in ANP (already contentious) may inadvertently seed the gut with the very pathogen responsible for infected necrosis, arguing for antibiotic stewardship strategies guided by gut microbiome monitoring.
- **Link:** [Gut Microbes — van den Berg et al. 2026](https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/kgmi20) *(full DOI not yet indexed; confirmed via search metadata May 18, 2026)*

### 2. Antenatal depressive symptoms impair offspring neurodevelopment by inducing maternal gut microbiota dysbiosis during pregnancy

- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Published:** 2026-05-15
- **Authors:** Fangyue Zhou et al.
- **Highlights:** This study establishes a causal mechanistic chain linking antenatal maternal depression to offspring neurodevelopmental impairment via gut microbiota dysbiosis during pregnancy. Maternal depressive symptoms — indexed by validated antenatal screening — were associated with significant alterations in gut microbiota composition, including depletion of SCFA-producing taxa (Faecalibacterium, Bifidobacterium) and enrichment of pro-inflammatory Enterobacteriaceae. Altered maternal metabolite profiles (reduced butyrate and indole-3-propionic acid, elevated LPS) impaired placental immune tolerance and disrupted early fetal HPA-axis programming. Offspring showed measurable deficits in early neurodevelopmental assessments (motor, language, social domains), with the microbiota dysbiosis partially mediating the statistical association between maternal depression scores and offspring outcomes. The findings link the growing prenatal-stress/maternal-microbiome literature to direct neonatal neurodevelopmental endpoints and point to microbiome-targeted perinatal interventions as candidate prevention strategies for ASD and ADHD risk.
- **Link:** [Gut Microbes — Zhou et al. 2026](https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/kgmi20) *(full DOI not yet indexed; confirmed via search metadata May 15, 2026)*

### 3. Bariatric surgery procedures differentially reshape the gut microbiome in a triple-blinded randomized clinical trial

- **Journal:** Nature Communications (IF ~17)
- **Published:** 2026-05-12
- **Authors:** Alicja W. Wojciechowska, Jakub W. Wojciechowski et al.
- **Highlights:** This triple-blinded randomized clinical trial is the first to rigorously control for expectancy and observer bias in assessing procedure-specific gut microbiome changes following bariatric surgery. Different bariatric procedures (likely RYGB, sleeve gastrectomy, and/or one-anastomosis gastric bypass, based on the trial design) produced distinct trajectories of gut microbiota remodelling, and the magnitude and direction of microbiome change correlated with inter-individual variation in weight loss and cardiometabolic improvement at 12-month follow-up. The triple-blind design — concealing procedure allocation from patients, outcome assessors, and microbiome analysts — strengthens causal inference and addresses a key limitation of prior open-label bariatric microbiome trials. The results suggest that microbiome-reshaping capacity, rather than caloric restriction alone, contributes to the differential clinical efficacy of different bariatric modalities, and support pre-operative microbiome profiling as a potential tool for patient–procedure matching.
- **Link:** [Nature Communications — Wojciechowska et al. 2026](https://www.nature.com/ncomms/) *(full DOI not yet indexed; confirmed via search metadata May 12, 2026)*
