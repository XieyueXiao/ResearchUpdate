## Papers Found (2026-07-18)

### 1. Multi-omic modelling of body mass index response to a dietary weight loss intervention
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Emily N. Yeo et al. (University of Colorado Boulder, Integrative Microbiome Research Lab) build multi-omic models integrating diet, gut bacterial species, and urine metabolites to predict individual BMI/weight-loss response to a dietary intervention. Reported models achieved ROC-AUC 0.84–0.88 for predicting weight-loss response and flagged a subset of likely non-responders with ~80% confidence, pointing toward microbiome/metabolome-informed personalization of dietary weight-loss programs. Published online 2026-07-15; open access.
