### 1. Dietary sulfur amino acids enhance anti-tumor immunity in colon cancer via an NKT cell–XCL1–cDC1 circuit

- **Journal:** Immunity (Cell Press; IF ~32) — *bonus; outside the 23-target list*
- **Highlights:** Lobel, Fonseca-Pereira, and colleagues (Broad Institute / Weizmann Institute / MD Anderson) show that dietary sulfur amino acid (SAA) content reshapes the colonic microbiome in a way that controls anti-tumor immunity in colon cancer. Sulfur amino acid metabolic pathways were enriched in metagenomes of immune-checkpoint-inhibitor (ICI) responders and in individuals without colon cancer, motivating mechanistic follow-up. In murine colon cancer models, high-SAA diets restricted tumor progression, increased colonic mucus thickness, and drove selective expansion of the mucus-dwelling commensal *Mucispirillum schaedleri*. Mechanistically, *M. schaedleri* stimulated natural killer T (NKT) cell expansion and induced secretion of the chemokine XCL1, which was essential for recruitment and activation of type-1 conventional dendritic cells (cDC1s) in tumor-draining lymph nodes; this NKT–XCL1–cDC1 axis was required for effective CD8+ T cell anti-tumor responses. High-SAA diets also increased the frequency of activated intratumoral cytotoxic CD8+ T cells. Dietary SAA supplementation in combination with ICIs may offer a readily testable clinical strategy for improving colorectal cancer immunotherapy outcomes.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1016/j.immuni.2026.05.001](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.immuni.2026.05.001)
