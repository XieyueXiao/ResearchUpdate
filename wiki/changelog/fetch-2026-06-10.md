## Papers Found (2026-06-10)

### 1. Commensal-derived acetylcholine enhances mucosal immune education

- **Journal:** Nature (IF ~64)
- **Highlights:** Song, Duncan-Lowey, Khetrapal et al. demonstrate that infant gut microbiomes are dominated by acetylcholine (ACh)-producing Bifidobacterium species that convert dietary choline into ACh. The bacterial enzymes mediating this biotransformation were identified in *B. breve* and the probiotic *Pediococcus pentosaceus*, and an isogenic ACh-null *B. breve* mutant was generated as a functional control. Mice colonised with ACh-producing *B. breve* showed markedly enhanced intestinal IgA production, an altered microbiota composition, and increased resistance to enteric infection compared with mutant-colonised mice. The findings define a diet→microbiome→ACh→mucosal-immunity axis in early life and provide a mechanistic explanation for why infant microbiomes rich in Bifidobacterium are associated with stronger gut barrier function and lower enteric infection risk.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1038/s41586-026-10592-7](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-026-10592-7)

### 2. An ubiquitous antibacterial toxin from human gut bacteria engenders neonatal colonization advantage

- **Journal:** Nature Communications (IF ~17)
- **Highlights:** Valguarnera, Tung, and Bubeck Wardenburg show that *Bacteroides fragilis* uses a Type VI Secretion System (T6SS) effector called Bte3 — the most prevalent T6SS effector in clinical human isolates — to eliminate competing *B. fragilis* strains and *Enterobacteriaceae* in vitro and in neonatal colonisation models. Bte3 functions as a periplasmic-intoxicating effector that employs previously uncharacterised adaptor proteins for secretion; T6SS-engineered strains carrying bte3-based toxin combinations consistently displaced competing strains lacking immunity genes. The results reveal an interbacterial warfare mechanism that shapes early microbiome assembly, with implications for neonatal colonisation dynamics and strategies to steer the infant gut microbiome.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1038/s41467-026-74062-4](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-026-74062-4)
