## Papers Found (2026-07-04)

### 1. Early risk stratification of late-onset sepsis in very preterm infants by intestinal microbiota profiling: a multicenter case–control validation study
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~15)
- **Highlights:** Late-onset sepsis (LOS) remains a leading cause of morbidity/mortality in very preterm infants, and existing clinical risk scores act late in the disease course. Frerichs et al. profiled longitudinal intestinal microbiota in a multicenter case–control cohort to test whether pre-onset microbiota signatures can flag LOS risk earlier than current clinical markers. The validation cohort confirms that specific compositional shifts (e.g., depletion of health-associated commensals paralleling prior LOS-microbiome literature) precede clinical sepsis onset and can be combined into a risk-stratification signature. Significance: supports intestinal microbiota profiling as an adjunct early-warning tool for neonatal ICU sepsis surveillance, potentially enabling earlier intervention in very preterm infants.

### 2. Multi-omics analysis of Saccharomyces boulardii supplementation reveals coordinated microbiome, metabolic, and immune signaling changes accompanying tumor suppression
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~15)
- **Highlights:** Vaaben et al. supplemented a tumor model system with the probiotic yeast Saccharomyces boulardii and applied multi-omics profiling (microbiome composition, metabolomics, immune signaling readouts) to characterize the mechanisms behind its previously reported tumor-suppressive effects. The analysis links coordinated shifts across gut microbial community structure, metabolite pools, and host immune signaling pathways to reduced tumor burden, extending mechanistic understanding of S. boulardii beyond earlier single-omics work (e.g., EGFR-signaling inhibition in Apc^min mice) toward a systems-level, multi-omics-integrated model. Significance: strengthens the case for S. boulardii as a mechanistically characterized adjunct in oncology-microbiome intervention research.
