### 1. Inorganic Nitrogen Metabolic Reprogramming of the Gut Microbiome Drives Fecal Microbiota Transplantation in Ulcerative Colitis

- **Journal:** Nature Communications (IF ~17)
- **Highlights:** FMT achieves remission in only a subset of UC patients, with poor donor microbiota engraftment as the primary bottleneck. Wang, Hou, Lv et al. identify inorganic nitrogen utilization capacity (IN-uc) as a critical gating factor: in the high-oxidative-stress, reactive-nitrogen-species (RNS)-rich gut environment of active UC, only donor taxa with sufficient IN-uc can establish themselves. The team engineered PM-mix14, a probiotic-metabolite consortia designed to boost IN-uc in the recipient gut by catalyzing RNS conversion, stimulating L-glutamate biosynthesis, and restoring ATP production — collectively lowering the colonization barrier for incoming donor microbiota. The study reframes FMT optimisation around nitrogen-cycle metabolism rather than community composition matching alone, with therapeutic implications for improving FMT engraftment rates in IBD.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1038/s41467-026-73290-y](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-026-73290-y)

### 2. Limosilactobacillus reuteri Promotes Melatonin Release from Human Intestinal Organoids via 5′-Ectonucleotidase Activity

- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Forshee et al. use human intestinal organoids to show that *Limosilactobacillus reuteri* directly stimulates melatonin secretion from gut epithelial cells through 5′-ectonucleotidase (CD73) activity, implicating a purinergic signalling intermediate in microbiota-driven melatonin regulation. Gut-derived melatonin is increasingly recognised as a key local mediator of circadian rhythm entrainment, epithelial barrier integrity, and mucosal immune tone; this work establishes a mechanistic link between a well-characterised probiotic commensal and the gut melatonin axis. Findings raise the possibility that probiotic administration could be used to correct circadian gut-epithelial dysfunction in conditions such as IBS, IBD, or shift-work-associated gut disorders.
- **Link:** DOI pending indexing; published online ~2026-05-18 in *Gut Microbes* Vol. 18 (2026)
