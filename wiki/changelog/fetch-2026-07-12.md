## Papers Found (2026-07-12)

### 1. Non-absorbable antibiotics worsen alcohol-associated liver disease in gastric acid-suppressed mice
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Fernanda Raya Tonetti et al. investigate how gastric acid suppression (a common comorbid exposure in alcohol-associated liver disease, ALD) interacts with non-absorbable antibiotic treatment to shape gut microbiota and liver outcomes in a mouse model of chronic ethanol feeding. Using gastric acid-suppressed mice, the authors show that non-absorbable antibiotics — often assumed to be a "safe" way to deplete gut bacteria therapeutically — instead worsen liver injury markers under these conditions, implicating a paradoxical dysbiosis/overgrowth effect rather than the expected protective reduction in bacterial translocation. The work signals caution for antibiotic-based microbiota-targeted strategies in ALD patients who are concurrently on acid-suppressive therapy (e.g., proton pump inhibitors), a very common clinical combination.
- **Link:** [Gut Microbes, published online 2026-07-08](https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/kgmi20) (DOI pending indexing at time of fetch)
