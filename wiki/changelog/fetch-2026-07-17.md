## Papers Found (2026-07-17)

### 1. Longitudinal modelling of microbiome subcommunities reveals parity-dependent dynamics during pregnancy and postpartum
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** K. M. Kennedy et al. apply longitudinal subcommunity modelling to track maternal gut microbiome dynamics across pregnancy and the postpartum period, using compositional subcommunity structure rather than single-taxon trajectories. The analysis finds that the pace and pattern of microbiome remodeling differ by parity (first vs. later pregnancies), suggesting prior pregnancy history leaves a lasting signature that shapes how the maternal gut microbiota responds to subsequent pregnancies.
