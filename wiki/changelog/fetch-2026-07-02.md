## Papers Found (2026-07-02)

### 1. Identification of human DP8α regulatory T cell sub-populations reactive to health-associated anti-inflammatory gut commensals
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Follow-up characterization of the FoxP3-negative, IL-10-secreting DP8α (CD4+CD8α+) regulatory T cell population previously shown to be induced by the gut commensal *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii* and reduced in inflammatory bowel disease. The study resolves DP8α Tregs into distinct sub-populations and extends TCR-reactivity profiling beyond *F. prausnitzii* to a broader panel of health-associated, anti-inflammatory gut commensals, refining the mechanistic link between specific keystone commensals and induction of this regulatory subset. Relevant to colonization-resistance/immune-modulation threads and to `wiki/organisms/` coverage of *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii*. Authors: Margaux de Seilhac et al.
- **Link:** Gut Microbes (Taylor & Francis) — published online 2026-06-28; DOI not yet resolvable via indexed search (pending full indexing)

### 2. Microbiome and metabolites impact enteric and central nervous systems in ALS
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Review synthesizing evidence that gut dysbiosis and altered microbial metabolite output (reduced SCFAs, nicotinamide, GABA, and serotonin precursors; increased intestinal permeability) act on both the enteric nervous system and the central nervous system in amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS). Integrates rodent SOD1/TDP-43 model data (e.g., butyrate reversing enteric neuromuscular deficits) with human cohort findings, framing the gut–enteric neuron–CNS metabolite axis as a candidate therapeutic target in ALS. Extends the knowledge base's gut-brain axis scope to a neurodegenerative disease context not previously covered. Authors: Erykah I. Walton et al.
- **Link:** Gut Microbes (Taylor & Francis) — published online 2026-06-29; DOI not yet resolvable via indexed search (pending full indexing)

### 3. Is Fusobacterium nucleatum the key mediator between oral infections and systemic diseases? Mechanistic insights and therapeutic implications
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Review positioning *Fusobacterium nucleatum* as a transboundary pathobiont linking oral dysbiosis to systemic disease (colorectal cancer, IBD, adverse pregnancy outcomes, cardiovascular disease), covering colonization/invasion mechanisms (FadA adhesin, ADP-heptose/ALPK1-TIFA signaling), hematogenous and oral-gut translocation routes, and emerging therapeutic strategies (targeted antimicrobials, adhesin-blocking approaches). Complements existing CRC/pathobiont pages (e.g., colibactin/EMT and Hippo-YAP *E. coli* mechanisms captured 2026-07-01) with a second major CRC-associated pathobiont mechanism. Authors: Gautham Siddarth Ragunagam et al.
- **Link:** Gut Microbes (Taylor & Francis) — published online 2026-06-30; DOI not yet resolvable via indexed search (pending full indexing)
