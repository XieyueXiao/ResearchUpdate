### 1. Computational and Systems Biology Approaches to Modelling Gut Microbiota

- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** This review article surveys the current landscape of computational and systems biology methods applied to understanding gut microbiota dynamics and function. It covers ecological modelling frameworks (e.g., Lotka-Volterra variants, agent-based models), genome-scale metabolic models of individual taxa and microbial communities, and multi-omics integration pipelines. The authors evaluate how these approaches are being coupled with emerging AI/ML tools to move toward predictive, mechanistic models of microbiome–host interactions. The review identifies key gaps in data standardisation and model parameterisation that currently limit clinical translation of computational microbiome insights.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1080/19490976.2026.2648xxx](https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/kgmi20) *(DOI not yet fully indexed; published online 2026-05-22)*

### 2. Exploring Differences in the Human Gut Microbiome Between Han Chinese and Non-Chinese Populations

- **Journal:** Genome Biology (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Wang, Zhang, Chen et al. performed a systematic large-scale analysis of gut microbial differences between Han Chinese and non-Chinese populations using the Human Gut Microbiome Reference Genome Catalog (HGMRGC), combining metagenomic abundance profiling with single-nucleotide polymorphism (SNP)-level population genomics. Geography emerged as the dominant driver of microbial variation at both the abundance and SNP levels, exceeding the contribution of host genetics. The researchers identified 689 population-specific genome clusters from the *Collinsella* genus carrying functional differences in carbohydrate utilisation pathways, and 108 species with distinct prevalence patterns linked to vitamin biosynthesis, antibiotic resistance gene carriage, and carbohydrate metabolism. The findings provide a high-resolution reference for dissecting how population-level factors shape gut microbiome structure and function, with implications for biomarker discovery and precision microbiome-based interventions across diverse ethnic groups.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1186/s13059-026-03932-3](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s13059-026-03932-3)
