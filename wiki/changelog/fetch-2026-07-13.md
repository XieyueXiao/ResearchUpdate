## Papers Found (2026-07-13)

### 1. A virulent bacterial signature is associated with the development of recurrence following colorectal cancer surgery
- **Journal:** Nature Communications (IF ~17)
- **Highlights:** Shogan, B.D. et al. profile the gut/tumor-site microbiome of colorectal cancer (CRC) patients on the day of surgery and show that a "virulent" bacterial signature present at that time is associated with subsequent development of cancer recurrence — up to one-third of CRC surgery patients recur post-resection, and this work links perioperative microbial community structure/virulence-gene burden to that risk. Frames the perioperative microbiome as a potential prognostic biomarker and a mechanistic contributor (rather than bystander) to CRC recurrence, distinct from prior CRC-microbiome-signature work that focuses on pre-treatment diagnostic association rather than post-surgical recurrence risk.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1038/s41467-026-74889-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-026-74889-x) (published 2026-07-06)
