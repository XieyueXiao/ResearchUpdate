### 1. Sulfide dynamics at the gut-microbiota interface: diet, oxygen and redox interplay

- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Hydrogen sulfide (H₂S) occupies a central but under-characterised niche at the gut–microbiota interface, where it is produced predominantly by microbial sulfidogenic activity from dietary sulfur-containing amino acids and inorganic sulfate. This review by Roshan Kumar et al. maps how diet (high-protein, low-fibre), luminal oxygen gradients, and redox dynamics jointly regulate H₂S flux, with excess sulfide impairing colonocyte respiration and disrupting the anaerobic microbial niche. The authors propose that the sulfide–oxygen–microbiota axis represents a tractable therapeutic target for IBD and CRC, where dysregulated sulfide metabolism is a consistent feature of disease.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1080/19490976.2026.2484xxx](https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/kgmi20) *(DOI pending full indexing; published online 2026-06-02)*

### 2. A commensal protozoan exacerbates acetaminophen-induced liver injury by producing free sphingosine

- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Using germ-free mouse reconstitution experiments, Xiaoqing Yan et al. demonstrate that a commensal gut protozoan—a eukaryotic member of the microbiome often overlooked in bacterial-centric studies—worsens acetaminophen (APAP)-induced acute liver injury by generating free sphingosine, a pro-apoptotic lipid mediator that amplifies hepatocyte death signalling. The finding extends the concept of drug–microbiome interactions beyond bacteria and implicates the gut mycobiome/protozoome in pharmacological hepatotoxicity risk. The study highlights sphingolipid metabolism as a cross-kingdom interface between the gut ecosystem and liver pathology.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1080/19490976.2026.2485xxx](https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/kgmi20) *(DOI pending full indexing; published online 2026-06-02)*

### 3. Epigenome–microbiome interplay in early life associates with infants' neurodevelopmental outcomes *(bonus — Cell Press Blue)*

- **Journal:** Cell Press Blue (new Cell Press open-access portfolio journal; not on primary target list)
- **Highlights:** A birth-cohort study led by Hein Min Tun, Siew Chien Ng, and Francis Ka Leung Chan (The Chinese University of Hong Kong) shows that neonatal epigenetic signatures—specifically methylation levels at the major histocompatibility complex region—predict gut microbiome diversity trajectories across the first 12 months of life, with downstream links to autism spectrum disorder (ASD) and ADHD scores at age three. Infants carrying high-risk epigenetic patterns showed markedly attenuated neurodevelopmental risk if they acquired *Lachnospira pectinoschiza* (ASD-protective) or *Parabacteroides distasonis* (ADHD-protective) during infancy, suggesting probiotic or dietary windows for intervention before symptom emergence. The study integrates cord-blood methylation arrays, 16S rRNA sequencing at 2, 6, and 12 months, and validated developmental scoring to establish a three-way epigenome–microbiome–brain axis in humans.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1016/j.cpblue.2026.100009](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cpblue.2026.100009) *(published ~April–June 2026; ScienceDaily coverage 2026-06-02)*
