## Papers Found (2026-07-22)

### 1. Peptide LKLKLL is a more effective component of Akkermansia muciniphila which regulate glucolipid metabolism through GLP-1/GIP dual modulation
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Yuan Li et al. investigate bioactive peptide components of *Akkermansia muciniphila*, building on prior work identifying whole-protein and postbiotic Akkermansia effectors of host metabolism. The study reports that the short peptide LKLKLL, derived from *A. muciniphila*, is a more potent driver of glucolipid metabolism regulation than previously characterized components, acting through dual modulation of both GLP-1 and GIP incretin signaling.
