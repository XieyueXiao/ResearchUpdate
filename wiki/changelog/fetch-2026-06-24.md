### 1. Biofilm overproduction enhances gastrointestinal stress tolerance and intestinal fitness in Bacillus subtilis

- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Kunyeit et al. examined whether the capacity to produce robust biofilms gives the spore-forming probiotic candidate *Bacillus subtilis* a survival advantage throughout the harsh physicochemical gradient of the gastrointestinal tract. Using biofilm-overproducing and biofilm-defective *B. subtilis* mutants, the authors assessed tolerance to acid, bile salts, and oxidative stress in vitro, and gut fitness in a *Caenorhabditis elegans* colonisation model. Biofilm-overproducing strains showed markedly enhanced tolerance to all GI stressors tested and out-competed wild-type and biofilm-defective strains for intestinal colonisation. The findings reveal biofilm formation as a previously underappreciated determinant of probiotic engraftment and persistence, with implications for engineering next-generation spore-forming probiotics with improved gut fitness properties.
