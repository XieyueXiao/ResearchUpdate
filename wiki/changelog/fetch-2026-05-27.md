### 1. Activity-guided substructure prioritization accelerates discovery of gut microbiota-derived immune-regulating metabolites
- **Journal:** Nature Communications (IF ~17)
- **Highlights:** Gut microbiota exert broad immunomodulatory effects partly through indole derivatives that activate the aryl hydrocarbon receptor (AhR), yet most microbiome-derived AhR ligands remain structurally uncharacterised. Zhao, Chi, Zhang et al. developed a substructure–activity relationship mass spectrometry (SAR-MS) pipeline that scores spectral features for structural similarity to bioactive scaffolds, then uses targeted activity screening to confirm AhR agonism. Applying the pipeline to a bacterial metabolite library, the team identified 22 metabolites carrying activity-related substructures, four of which were entirely novel; the characterised sulfonated and methylthiolated indole compounds represent two previously unrecognised classes of microbiome-mediated chemical reactions. The work demonstrates a broadly applicable activity-guided metabolomics strategy for bridging the gap between microbiome-associated mass-spectral features and functionally annotated immune-regulating metabolites.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1038/s41467-026-73030-2](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-026-73030-2)
