### 1. Gut microbiome features associated with Bifidobacterium colonization predict personalized probiotic persistence patterns

- **Journal:** Nature Communications (IF ~17)
- **Highlights:** Goswami, Ansari, Saraf et al. analyzed 51,244 gut microbiomes drawn from 149 cohorts across 45 countries to map the ecological context of Bifidobacterial colonization in the human gut. Using Association-Scores derived from co-occurrence patterns, they identified consistent positive associations between multiple Bifidobacterium species and butyrate-producing Firmicutes (including Faecalibacterium prausnitzii and Roseburia spp.) and Collinsella spp., and consistent negative associations with certain pathobiont-Firmicutes and Bacteroidota taxa. These per-species profiles were aggregated into microbiome-level "Receptive Scores" that quantify how permissive a baseline microbiome is to colonization by a given Bifidobacterium probiotic. In external validation across eight independent intervention studies (1,633 gut microbiomes), combining a participant's baseline Bifidobacterium abundance with the corresponding baseline Receptive Score significantly predicted post-supplementation persistence or expansion in 69–82% of trial-probiotic pairs — substantially outperforming abundance alone. The framework offers a practical pre-treatment screening tool to personalize Bifidobacterial probiotic prescribing and may explain much of the inter-individual variation in clinical probiotic trials.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1038/s41467-026-72289-9](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-026-72289-9)
