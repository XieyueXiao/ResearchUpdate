### 1. The infant gut virome assembles rapidly and predictably over the first three years of life

- **Journal:** Nature Communications (IF ~17)
- **Highlights:** Maurice et al. performed a meta-analysis of 12 infant cohorts across 8 countries (published June 18, 2026) to model gut virome development over the first three years of life. Despite individual variation at the sequence level, the infant gut virome follows a predictable, conserved developmental programme: viral richness increases significantly over the first eight months of life, virome developmental velocity (the rate of change between sequential time-point samples) decreases over time, and community-wide convergence occurs as infant viromes become progressively more similar with age, stabilising by approximately two years. The findings establish baseline expectations for healthy early-life virome maturation and provide a framework for identifying disrupted virome development in clinical cohorts.
- **Link:** [doi pending full indexing — preprint: 10.64898/2026.02.17.705938](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.02.17.705938v1.full)

### 2. Host factors dictate gut microbiome alterations in chronic kidney disease more strongly than kidney function

- **Journal:** Nature Microbiology (IF ~28)
- **Highlights:** Combining quantitative fecal metagenomics from 130 CKD patients with cross-study biomarker comparisons across 4,420 individuals, this study found that intestinal transit time (ITT) and medication exposure explained far more variance in gut microbiome composition than eGFR-related kidney function. Lower eGFR was associated with increased p-cresol and indole biosynthetic potential and reduced plant-to-animal carbohydrate-active enzyme ratios, consistent with microbial transitions from saccharolytic to proteolytic metabolism driven by slowed ITT rather than uremia per se. Peritoneal dialysis patients showed distinct dysbiosis with increased intestinal inflammation. The findings redirect therapeutic targeting in CKD-associated dysbiosis away from kidney biomarkers and toward host physiological modulators of the gut environment.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1038/s41564-026-02259-w](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41564-026-02259-w)

### 3. Gut microbial markers of immunotherapy response in melanoma: a cross-cohort analysis including the first Russian dataset

- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Strokach et al. (published June 15, 2026) performed a cross-cohort metagenomic analysis of gut microbial markers predictive of immune checkpoint inhibitor (ICI) response in advanced melanoma, incorporating the first Russian patient dataset alongside Western cohorts. By analysing stool metagenomes across multiple cohorts including naive ICI and FMT-combined treatment arms, the authors identified a set of consistently enriched taxa in responders and applied cross-cohort validation to separate robust biomarkers from cohort-specific signals. The study adds geographic and ethnic diversity to melanoma–microbiome biomarker discovery and highlights the importance of multi-population replication for translating gut microbial signatures into clinical tools.
- **Link:** [Gut Microbes, online June 15, 2026 — doi pending](https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/kgmi20)

### 4. Recent advances and clinical relevance of microbiome dynamics in health and disease

- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Gavanji et al. (published June 15, 2026; doi:10.1080/19490976.2026.2679197) provide a comprehensive review of how temporal and contextual shifts in gut microbiome composition — shaped by diet, geography, ageing, and medication exposure — translate into clinically measurable outcomes. The review covers beneficial commensal functions (immune tolerance, metabolic balance, complex carbohydrate fermentation, vitamin biosynthesis), the microbiome perturbations caused by antibiotics, proton-pump inhibitors, and ultra-processed diets, and the downstream consequences of dysbiosis across gastrointestinal, metabolic, and neurological disease domains. Particular emphasis is placed on the translational gap between population-level microbiome characterisation and personalised intervention strategies.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1080/19490976.2026.2679197](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/19490976.2026.2679197)

### 5. Meta-analytic microbiome target discovery for immune checkpoint inhibitor response in advanced melanoma [BONUS — Communications Medicine]

- **Journal:** Communications Medicine / Nature portfolio (not on primary target list)
- **Highlights:** Zhang, Mallick & Rahnavard (Communications Medicine vol. 6, article 298; doi:10.1038/s43856-026-01612-8) reprocessed stool metagenomic sequencing data from 15 melanoma ICI cohorts (763 samples from 484 individuals), including 12 cohorts treated with ICI alone and 3 FMT-combined trials, profiling microbial species, metabolic pathways, and biosynthetic gene clusters. The meta-analysis demonstrates that microbiome signatures of ICI response are treatment-context dependent: no single universal species reliably predicts response across all treatment modalities, and FMT-combined arms show different microbial correlates than ICI-monotherapy arms. The work underscores the need for treatment-stratified microbiome biomarker frameworks and motivates multi-cohort harmonisation approaches.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1038/s43856-026-01612-8](https://www.nature.com/articles/s43856-026-01612-8)
