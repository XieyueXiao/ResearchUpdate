## Papers Found (2026-07-01)

### 1. Emergence of invasive Escherichia coli pathobionts in gut microbiome promotes cancer stemness via targeting Hippo pathways
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Colonic pathobiont *E. coli* strains isolated from tumor-adjacent mucosa were characterized for invasive/colitogenic phenotypes and tested in colon epithelial and organoid models. Invasive *E. coli* colonization suppressed Hippo pathway signaling (reduced LATS1/2 activity), driving nuclear YAP/TAZ accumulation and expansion of a cancer-stem-cell-like epithelial population. The findings mechanistically link a specific gut pathobiont lineage to a defined oncogenic signaling axis, extending prior colibactin/EMT-focused models of *E. coli*-driven colorectal carcinogenesis to Hippo-YAP signaling and stemness induction. Authors: Linda Chia-Hui Yu et al.
- **Link:** [Gut Microbes (Taylor & Francis)](https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/kgmi20) — published online 2026-06-30; DOI not yet resolvable via indexed search (pending full indexing)

### 2. Perinatal ampicillin exposure alters murine maternal fecal bile acid and acylcarnitine profiles
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** In a murine model of perinatal antibiotic exposure (intrapartum-analogous ampicillin dosing), maternal fecal shotgun metagenomics and untargeted metabolomics showed reproducible depletion of Muribaculaceae (including *Muribaculum intestinale* and *Duncaniella dubosii*) alongside cohort-dependent enrichment of *Enterococcus* and *Prevotella*. Metabolic remodeling included elevated fecal acylcarnitines and dysregulated primary/secondary bile acid profiles, with two previously uncharacterized trihydroxylated, hexose-conjugated bile acids identified as antibiotic-exposure-associated features. Maternal microbiome/metabolome shifts coincided with increased offspring weight gain, supporting a maternal-microbiome-mediated mechanism linking perinatal antibiotic exposure to altered early-life growth trajectories. Authors: Simone Zuffa et al.
- **Link:** [PMC12236745](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12236745) — published online 2026-06-29 (related bioRxiv preprint: [10.1101/2025.06.30.662372](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.06.30.662372v1))
