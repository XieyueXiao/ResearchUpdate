## Papers Found (2026-06-29)

### 1. Mucin-derived sugars act as metabolic brakes controlling growth initiation in Akkermansia muciniphila

- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Akkermansia muciniphila is a keystone mucus-layer resident whose abundance correlates strongly with host metabolic health, yet what controls its colonisation dynamics within the gut has remained unclear. Lakey et al. demonstrate that specific mucin-derived monosaccharides function not merely as nutrient substrates but as active regulatory signals that inhibit the onset of A. muciniphila growth — acting as "metabolic brakes" on growth initiation rather than simple carbon sources. The finding reframes how host mucin glycan composition shapes A. muciniphila colonisation levels, with implications for precision strategies to modulate this organism therapeutically through mucin substrate availability or dietary intervention.
- **Link:** [Gut Microbes — Lakey et al. 2026](https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/kgmi20) (DOI pending full indexing; published online 2026-06-27)

### 2. Early antibiotic exposure and vaccine immune responses in preterm infants: potential sex-specific differences

- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Preterm infants routinely receive broad-spectrum antibiotics in neonatal intensive care, profoundly disrupting the early gut microbiome during a critical window of immune programming. Haag et al. examine how this early antibiotic exposure affects vaccine immunogenicity in preterm neonates and report evidence of sex-specific differences in vaccine immune responses linked to antibiotic-driven microbiome perturbations. The study adds a sex-stratification dimension to the emerging literature on antibiotic–microbiome–vaccine axis interactions in early life, suggesting that male and female preterm infants may require different microbiome-informed vaccination strategies.
- **Link:** [Gut Microbes — Haag et al. 2026](https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/kgmi20) (DOI pending full indexing; published online 2026-06-27)
