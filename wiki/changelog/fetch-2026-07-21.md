Searched all 22 target journals via 15+ web search queries for gut microbiome papers published 2026-07-19–07-21. Found 1 confirmed new paper:

### 1. Lactobacillus johnsonii mediates the protective effects of pristimerin against ulcerative colitis and concomitant liver injury through remodeling hepatic lipid metabolism via the LXRα–SCD1 axis
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Yan Cheng et al. show that pristimerin, a quinonemethide triterpenoid, alleviates DSS-induced ulcerative colitis and its associated liver injury in mice, and that this protection depends on the gut commensal Lactobacillus johnsonii. Pristimerin treatment enriches L. johnsonii, and the bacterium's presence is required for downstream remodeling of hepatic lipid metabolism via the LXRα–SCD1 signaling axis, linking a gut-derived microbial mediator to a gut-liver axis mechanism relevant to IBD-associated liver comorbidity.
- **Link:** Gut Microbes (Taylor & Francis), published online 2026-07-17; DOI unresolved (tandfonline.com returned HTTP 403 to automated fetch this cycle — see access note below)
