## Papers Found (2026-07-14)

### 1. The primary sclerosing cholangitis and ulcerative colitis colonic mucosa defined through paired microbial and single-cell RNA sequencing
- **Journal:** Nature Communications (IF ~17)
- **Highlights:** Tearle, Zhang, Jackson, Malhotra et al. (corresponding author Kylie R. James) combine single-cell RNA/antigen-receptor sequencing, 16S microbiota profiling, and spatial transcriptomics on colonic mucosa biopsies from four anatomic regions to compare primary sclerosing cholangitis-associated colitis (PSC-UC) against conventional ulcerative colitis (UC). PSC-UC shows enrichment of CD8 T, γδ T, and NK cells alongside reduced microbial diversity in the right colon even without symptomatic inflammation — a pattern absent in UC — while during flare both diseases show expansion of a novel inflammatory mast cell state sustained via TMEM176B signaling. Frames PSC-UC as a molecularly and microbially distinct entity from UC despite shared histology, relevant to the elevated colitis-associated colorectal cancer risk seen in PSC-IBD; originated as a bioRxiv preprint (doi:10.1101/2024.08.12.607536, Aug 2024) and a Jan 2025 Journal of Crohn's and Colitis conference abstract (P0161), with the peer-reviewed Nature Communications version confirmed via independent search triangulation as published 2026-07-07.
- **Link:** DOI pending indexing (nature.com access blocked this cycle; preprint at [10.1101/2024.08.12.607536](https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.08.12.607536))
