### 1. Dolutegravir Restores Gut Microbiota in Late-Stage HIV-1 Unlike Darunavir: An Open-Label, Randomized Clinical Trial (ADVANZ-4 MISTRAL)

- **Journal:** Nature Communications (IF ~17)
- **Highlights:** The ADVANZ-4 MISTRAL trial enrolled 88 antiretroviral-naïve individuals with advanced HIV-1 infection (median baseline CD4⁺ T cells: 34 cells/mm³) and randomized them 1:1 to receive lamivudine/abacavir plus either dolutegravir (integrase strand-transfer inhibitor) or ritonavir-boosted darunavir (protease inhibitor), with 2-year follow-up. Both arms achieved comparable HIV-1 viral suppression and CD4⁺ recovery. However, only dolutegravir-treated participants showed significant restoration of gut microbial richness and alpha-diversity, with enrichment of beneficial commensal taxa and metabolic functional pathways (including amino acid biosynthesis and short-chain fatty acid metabolism); darunavir/ritonavir produced no such recovery. Microbiome restoration in the dolutegravir arm correlated strongly with reductions in systemic inflammation markers and immune activation indices. The study demonstrates that antiretroviral drug class choice — beyond viral suppression — determines whether the devastated gut ecosystem of advanced HIV disease recovers, and positions integrase inhibitors as preferable for microbiome-dependent immune reconstitution. An author correction was subsequently published (doi:10.1038/s41467-026-72458-w).
