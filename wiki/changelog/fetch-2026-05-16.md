### 1. Acarbose redirects gut microbiome utilization of dietary carbohydrates to suppress anaphylaxis in mice

- **Journal:** Nature Microbiology (IF ~28)
- **Published:** 2026-03-18
- **Highlights:** The widely prescribed antidiabetic α-glucosidase inhibitor acarbose blocks small-intestinal carbohydrate absorption, redirecting complex carbohydrates to the colon where they fuel the proliferation of *Parabacteroides distasonis*. This microbial shift elevates colonic succinate concentrations and intracellular NAD⁺ levels while reducing reliance on b-type cytochrome-dependent anaerobic respiration. Direct administration of succinate suppressed mast cell degranulation and systemic anaphylaxis in mice independently of adaptive immune responses; a human cohort analysis confirmed a lower incidence of anaphylaxis in patients receiving α-glucosidase inhibitors compared with untreated controls. The study establishes a diet–microbiome–metabolite axis as a druggable route for controlling IgE-independent anaphylaxis and positions *P. distasonis* succinate production as a key effector linking carbohydrate availability to mast cell tone.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1038/s41564-026-02350-2](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41564-026-02350-2)

### 2. Alternative antibiotic regimens improve palatability and welfare in mice for gut bacterial depletion

- **Journal:** Lab Animal (Nature portfolio)
- **Published:** ~2026-05-12
- **Highlights:** Standard broad-spectrum antibiotic cocktails used for gut microbiome depletion in mice are poorly tolerated, reducing food and water intake and confounding metabolic experiments. This study evaluated two alternative regimens — enrofloxacin–ampicillin and a second combination — both delivered in drinking water. Both were well tolerated with no significant effect on food intake or body weight, and metabolic profiling confirmed reduced bacterial metabolic activity in both groups. Critically, only the enrofloxacin–ampicillin regimen produced a marked reduction in total bacterial abundance comparable to the standard cocktail, making it the preferred option. Results validate enrofloxacin–ampicillin as a welfare-improving, scientifically equivalent strategy for gnotobiotic and microbiome-depletion mouse models.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1038/s41684-026-01728-3](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41684-026-01728-3)

### 3. Restoring a Youthful Gut Microbiome Prevents Liver Cancer and Reverses Hepatic Aging in Mice *(DDW 2026 Abstract)*

- **Journal:** Presented at Digestive Disease Week® (DDW) 2026 — not yet published in a target journal
- **Highlights:** Researchers at the University of Texas Medical Branch (Qingjie Li et al.) collected fecal microbiota from young mice and cryopreserved it; when these same mice were aged, autologous FMT using the young microbiome was performed. By end-of-study, none of the FMT-treated aged mice developed liver cancer, versus 2/8 untreated aging controls. Treated animals showed suppressed MDM2 protein levels (normally elevated in aged and cancerous liver tissue), reduced hepatic inflammation, and decreased liver injury markers — suggesting that MDM2 induction is a microbiome-dependent mechanism driving age-related hepatic carcinogenesis. The autologous design minimises immune complications and provides a proof-of-concept framework for future human trials.
- **Link:** [DDW 2026 abstract](https://news.ddw.org/news/restoring-a-youthful-gut-microbiome-prevents-liver-cancer-and-reverses-hepatic-aging-in-mice/)
