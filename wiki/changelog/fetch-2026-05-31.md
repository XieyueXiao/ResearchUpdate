### 1. Long-lasting gut microbiome and fecal metabolome alterations after colorectal adenoma removal and their relationship to colorectal cancer

- **Journal:** Cell Host & Microbe (IF ~30)
- **Highlights:** Colorectal adenoma (CRA) removal does not restore the gut microbiome to a healthy baseline — alterations persist for more than a decade and may actively sustain elevated colorectal cancer (CRC) risk. Researchers at Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health profiled stool metagenomes from 354 women sampled 12.1 ± 4.8 years after adenoma resection and from 1:1-matched adenoma-free controls, plus fecal metabolomes from 184 pairs. Thirty-one microbial species — including Faecalibacterium prausnitzii and Flavonifractor plautii — were significantly altered in both adenoma history and CRC, all correlating with lifestyle factors such as diet and physical activity. Thirty fecal metabolites and 7 sub-pathways (most prominently sphingolipids) were associated with adenoma history; disease-specific microbe–metabolite links included Bilophila wadsworthia co-occurring with alanine-containing dipeptides. Notably, diet and physical activity explained more variance in CRC-associated microbe abundances within the adenoma-history group than in adenoma-free controls, implicating modifiable lifestyle as a lever for post-polypectomy microbiome remodelling and secondary CRC prevention.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1016/j.chom.2026.05.001](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.chom.2026.05.001)
