## Papers Found (2026-05-13)

### 1. Gut microbes mediate the synergistic effects of dietary cholesterol and saturated fat in driving fibrosing MASH
- **Journal:** Gut Microbes (IF ~12)
- **Highlights:** Using specific pathogen-free (SPF) and germ-free (GF) mouse models fed diets independently and jointly enriched in saturated fat and cholesterol, Hermanson et al. demonstrate that both dietary components must be present simultaneously to induce fibrosing metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH); GF mice were fully protected under the combined diet, establishing gut microbiota as an obligate mediator. Individual dietary components altered microbial membership via changes in bile acid metabolism, while the combined cholesterol + saturated fat diet drove a distinct dysbiotic community notably enriched in Parasutterella spp. The study directly implicates a diet–microbiome–bile acid axis in progression from MASLD to fibrosing MASH, providing a mechanistic framework for microbiome-targeted MASH prevention.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1080/19490976.2026.2382336](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/19490976.2026.2382336) *(preprint: [bioRxiv 2025.07.16.665145](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.07.16.665145v1.full))*

### 2. Next-generation probiotics: an outlook into current applications and future developments
- **Journal:** Nature Reviews Microbiology (IF ~69)
- **Highlights:** Kern, Tofield, Frame, and Elinav (Weizmann Institute) provide an authoritative comprehensive review of next-generation probiotics (NGPs) — health-associated commensals identified through large-scale microbiome association studies rather than by traditional empirical selection. The review covers clinical evidence for leading NGP candidates (Akkermansia muciniphila, Faecalibacterium prausnitzii, Christensenella minuta, Eubacterium hallii), synthetic biology and programmable gene-circuit engineering approaches for enhanced therapeutic function, and paths from candidate identification to clinical-grade manufacturing. A dedicated section addresses AI-assisted personalisation of probiotic regimens based on individual microbiome profiles; regulatory bottlenecks and long-term safety of engineered strains are highlighted as key hurdles for widespread therapeutic deployment.
- **Link:** [doi:10.1038/s41579-026-01311-0](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41579-026-01311-0)
